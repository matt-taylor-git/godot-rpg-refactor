# Opening Cinematic Visual Identity

## Style Prompt

A restrained dark-fantasy prologue built from the game's existing illustrated world: an old parchment map, a moonlit forest, an ember-lit mountain pass, and a lone torchbearer before an ancient gateway. The imagery should feel like pages of a weathered chronicle coming alive under warm upper-left candlelight. Motion is slow, weighty, and atmospheric, using controlled camera drift, fog, embers, bronze geometry, and soft focus transitions. The composition is intentionally title-neutral and contains no game name or baked-in narrative copy.

## Colors

- Warm charcoal background: `#141210`
- Parchment light: `#F2EBD9`
- Deep bronze: `#59401A`
- Muted bronze edge: `#997333`
- Reserved gold highlight: `#D9B359`
- Ember orange: `#C8672A`

## Typography

- No text is embedded in the rendered cinematic.
- If editable overlays are added later in Godot, use Cinzel for short display text and Source Serif 4 for body copy.

## Motion

- Camera drift and breathing light are very slow and deliberate.
- Entrances use varied focus, scale, and directional reveals.
- Scene changes use one consistent blur-crossfade language.
- No sharp digital effects, elastic motion, or rapid cuts.
- The final gateway image holds steady so Godot can transition into the matching main-menu artwork.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not display the game name, a logo, or a placeholder title.
- Do not use pure white, pure black, neon colors, or cool tech effects.
- Do not add generic fantasy slogans or baked-in subtitles.
- Do not use geometric tile transitions or conspicuous repeating patterns.
- Do not overwhelm the illustrations with bright gold borders or excessive particles.
